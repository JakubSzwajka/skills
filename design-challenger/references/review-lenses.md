# Review Lenses

Use the lenses selectively. Do not dump all of them every time. Prioritize the ones with the biggest likely impact on comprehension, trust, and conversion.

## 1. Target audience fit

Ask:
- Does this feel made for the stated user, or for the team’s taste?
- Is the language, density, and framing right for their level of expertise?
- Does the screen answer the user’s likely concern fast enough?
- Is the emotional tone right: urgent, calm, premium, practical, playful?

Watch for:
- generic messaging
- aspirational styling with no audience grounding
- expert UI for novice users, or the reverse

## 2. Conversion path

Ask:
- What is the one primary action?
- Is the next step obvious?
- What friction or doubt stops the click, signup, purchase, or completion?
- Is the CTA visible, credible, and appropriately timed?

Watch for:
- competing CTAs
- hidden or soft primary action
- unclear value before asking for commitment
- too much effort too early

## 3. Visual hierarchy

Ask:
- What gets seen first, second, and third?
- Does that match what should matter most?
- Is emphasis earned or randomly distributed?

Watch for:
- too many loud elements
- headings that do not carry the message
- weak contrast between primary and secondary content
- decorative emphasis overpowering useful information

## 4. Information architecture

Ask:
- Is the content grouped in a way users can predict?
- Are related things together and unrelated things separated?
- Is the sequence logical?

Watch for:
- random section ordering
- missing transitions or context
- burying decision-critical information

## 5. Clarity of value proposition

Ask:
- Can a user explain what this product or step is for after a quick scan?
- Is the benefit concrete or abstract?
- Does the screen answer “why should I care?” quickly?

Watch for:
- slogan-first layouts
- feature lists without outcomes
- vague marketing copy that fails to reduce uncertainty

## 6. Trust and credibility

Ask:
- What makes this believable?
- What reduces hesitation?
- Does anything feel cheap, misleading, dated, or suspicious?

Watch for:
- low-quality visual polish near critical actions
- missing proof, examples, or constraints
- overclaiming without support
- dark-pattern energy

## 7. Consistency

Ask:
- Are repeated patterns actually repeated?
- Do components, spacing, labels, and actions behave predictably?
- Does this feel like one product or several ideas stitched together?

Watch for:
- inconsistent button hierarchy
- mixed spacing logic
- shifts in tone or component rules
- arbitrary exceptions that confuse users

## 8. Interaction friction

Ask:
- How much thinking is required at each step?
- Are there unnecessary choices, fields, or toggles?
- Does the UI help recovery from mistakes?

Watch for:
- too many decisions at once
- hidden dependencies
- unclear states
- weak feedback after actions

## 9. Forms and inputs

Ask:
- Are only necessary fields shown?
- Is the order natural?
- Are labels, help text, defaults, and validation doing enough work?

Watch for:
- placeholder-only labeling
- unclear required fields
- long forms with no progressive disclosure
- generic error handling

## 10. Content density and readability

Ask:
- Is there enough breathing room?
- Is the amount of content appropriate for the decision being made?
- Can users scan instead of read every word?

Watch for:
- wall-of-text sections
- weak chunking
- tiny type or overloaded cards
- too much whitespace with too little meaning

## 11. Accessibility and inclusive clarity

Ask:
- Would low-vision, keyboard, or cognitively overloaded users still succeed?
- Is meaning communicated by more than color?
- Are labels and controls explicit enough?

Watch for:
- weak contrast
- ambiguous icon-only actions
- tiny hit areas
- inaccessible error or status communication

## 12. Mobile and responsive behavior

Ask:
- What breaks first on a small screen?
- Does hierarchy survive when stacked?
- Are actions still obvious without desktop width?

Watch for:
- wide multi-column assumptions
- CTA disappearance below the fold
- giant hero blocks that delay action
- cramped cards and broken spacing
